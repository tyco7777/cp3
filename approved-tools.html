University‑Approved AI Tools

California Baptist University maintains a list of artificial intelligence tools that have been reviewed and approved by Information Technology Services (ITS) for university‑related work. Approved tools are selected to align with institutional security, privacy, and compliance requirements.

Approval of an AI tool does not imply unrestricted use. All AI use must comply with CBU policy, data‑classification standards, and ITS guidance.

CBU‑Preferred AI Platform

Microsoft Copilot (Microsoft 365) is CBU’s primary approved generative AI platform. Copilot is integrated into Microsoft 365 applications and operates within enterprise security, identity, and access‑control protections.

IT Review Process for New Tools or Pilots

Faculty, staff, or departments seeking to use AI tools not currently approved must submit a request for review through Information Technology Services before use. Reviews evaluate tools based on:

  • Data protection, privacy, and security controls
  • Legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations
  • Institutional risk and ethical considerations
  • Appropriateness of the proposed use case

Tools may be approved with conditions, limited to specific data classifications, or declined based on assessed risk.
