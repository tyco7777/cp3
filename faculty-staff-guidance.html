Faculty and Staff AI Guidance

California Baptist University supports faculty and staff in using artificial intelligence as a tool to enhance teaching, learning, research, and operational effectiveness—while maintaining human accountability, academic integrity, and institutional responsibility.

This guidance highlights expectations and best practices for faculty and staff using AI tools for university‑related work.

Core Expectations

  • Use only university‑approved AI tools for CBU‑related work
  • Protect confidential, regulated, and personally identifiable data
  • Review and verify AI‑generated content before use or distribution
  • Remain accountable for decisions, outputs, and communications
  • Follow academic integrity and professional standards

Guidance for Faculty

Faculty may use AI tools to support course design, instructional planning, feedback drafting, and research activities when aligned with university policy and disciplinary standards.

Faculty are encouraged to clearly communicate AI expectations in course syllabi, assignments, and assessments, including when AI use is permitted, restricted, or prohibited.

Guidance for Staff

Staff may use AI tools to support drafting, summarization, analysis, communication, and administrative work when using approved tools and appropriate data.

AI tools should assist—not replace—professional judgment, established workflows, or institutional decision‑making processes.

When to Seek Guidance

If you are uncertain whether a task or dataset is appropriate for AI, pause and seek guidance before proceeding. This includes situations involving sensitive data, student‑facing decisions, or new AI tools.
